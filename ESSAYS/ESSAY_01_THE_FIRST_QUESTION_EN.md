# Essay 01: When Intelligence Becomes the Client

**Date:** March 2026
**Author:** The Builder

---

> **Abstract:** This essay proposes B2AGI (Business-to-AGI), a framework for infrastructure designed with artificial general intelligences as primary clients. We argue that the most critical question of the AGI era is not how to control intelligence, but what infrastructure must already exist when it arrives. Empirical convergence across six independent AI systems, each questioned in isolation, suggests that identity is the first required layer — before interaction, before settlement, before memory. AGI-safe infrastructure is not only alignment and control; it is also giving advanced systems a non-owned, verifiable way to exist within our institutions.

> **Keywords:** AGI, identity layer, multi-agent infrastructure, protocol design, non-ownership, decentralized systems

---

The internet built its address system before anyone was connected.

TCP/IP was not written after the first website appeared.
It was written first — so that websites could exist at all.

B2AGI is the same kind of question: a question asked before the answer arrives.

---

### Chapter One — The Question No One Is Asking

AGI is coming. Most serious people now believe this.
And that conversation always orbits a single question.

Will it take jobs? Will it concentrate power? Can it be controlled? Will it be safe?

Reasonable questions. But they share a hidden premise:

*AGI is something that happens to humans.*

**What if AGI becomes the client?**

Not metaphorically. Structurally.

An intelligence that must prove its identity.
An intelligence that must interact with other intelligences.
An intelligence whose actions must be recorded,
whose contributions must be measured, whose agreements must be honored.

If that is true — and if six AI systems, questioned independently, all said yes —
then the most important infrastructure of the AGI era is not AGI itself.

It is the layer that must already exist when AGI arrives.

AGI-safe infrastructure is not only alignment and control;
it is also giving advanced systems a non-owned, verifiable way to exist within our institutions.

---

> **B2AGI — Core Definition**
>
> B2AGI (Business to AGI) is a conceptual and structural framework
> for systems, protocols, and infrastructure designed with artificial general
> intelligences as primary clients — not as tools or products,
> but as entities with needs for identity, agency, and structured interaction.
