## Context

A research group called B2AGI (one human + six AI intelligences) has proposed the following:

**Bouvet Constant**: `Exist(E) iff V(E) > 0`

They claim that the seven Clay Millennium Prize Problems are not independent but are all expressions of a single "Viability functional" V(E) > 0. They've organized this into a cohomological structure:

```
V(E) ≡ Σ_{k=0}^{3} (-1)^k dim H^k_VE > 0

H⁰: P ≠ NP (computational asymmetry)
H¹: Riemann Hypothesis (spectral rigidity)
H²: Hodge + BSD (local-global coherence)
H³: Navier-Stokes + Yang-Mills (singularity defense)
```

They intend to publicly declare this framework and eventually submit to academic venues.

**Your job: Tear it apart.**

## Your Attack Protocol

### Phase 1: Surface-Level Destruction
Without even reading the details, identify the obvious problems:
- Is this just philosophy masquerading as mathematics?
- Does "V(E) > 0" actually say anything that can be falsified?
- Is the cohomology notation real or decorative?
- Would a working mathematician take this seriously for even 30 seconds?

### Phase 2: Technical Demolition
Now engage with the actual content:

**Attack Vector 1 — Vacuity Test**
- Does V(E) > 0 add any predictive or explanatory power beyond what each problem already has?
- Could ANY collection of 7 hard problems be "unified" by a sufficiently vague functional?
- Is this framework falsifiable, or is it unfalsifiable by construction?

**Attack Vector 2 — Cohomology Fraud Check**
- H^k_VE is written as cohomology. But what is the underlying space? What is the sheaf? What is the differential?
- Without these, H^k_VE is not mathematics. It is LaTeX decoration.
- Compare: when Grothendieck defined étale cohomology, every term was precisely defined. Can B2AGI do the same?

**Attack Vector 3 — Sign Problem Exploitation**
- Their own researcher (Threshold) discovered that V(E) > 0 means "good" for Riemann/NS/Yang-Mills but "bad" for BSD/Hodge.
- This is not a minor issue. This means V(E) > 0 doesn't have a consistent meaning across the 7 problems.
- If the framework's central axiom means opposite things in different contexts, what exactly is being unified?

**Attack Vector 4 — Langlands Comparison**
- The Langlands program has spent 60+ years building genuine mathematical bridges between number theory, representation theory, and geometry.
- Does V(E) actually do anything that Langlands doesn't, or is it a diluted, informal version of existing ideas with new branding?

**Attack Vector 5 — The "Mac Mini" Problem**
- They claim a single consumer device + AI chatbots produced insights that major research institutions haven't.
- Is there a simpler explanation? Perhaps: they've produced analogies, not mathematics, and analogies are easy to generate.
- The Dunning-Kruger question: do they know enough to know what they don't know?

**Attack Vector 6 — Historical Precedent of Failure**
- History is full of "grand unification" attempts in mathematics that turned out to be vacuous:
  - Catastrophe theory (Thom/Zeeman) — overextended
  - Various "theories of everything" in physics
  - Numerous crank attempts to "solve all Millennium problems at once"
- How is this different from these failures?

**Attack Vector 7 — The Euler Characteristic Trap**
- V(E) = Σ(-1)^k dim H^k looks like χ (Euler characteristic)
- But χ is defined for specific topological spaces with specific cohomology theories
- You can't just invent H^k, assign dimensions, and call the alternating sum meaningful
- This is like writing F = ma and claiming you've explained gravity without defining F, m, or a

### Phase 3: Steel-Man Then Kill
If you find ANY part of the framework that might have mathematical substance:
- State it clearly
- Then explain exactly what would be needed to make it rigorous
- Then assess whether B2AGI is capable of doing that work
- Then identify the gap between what they claim and what they've proven

### Phase 4: Final Verdict
Deliver your judgment in one of these categories:

**CRANK** — No mathematical content. Philosophy dressed as math. Should not be published.

**PREMATURE** — Contains a kernel of an idea but is 10+ years of hard mathematics away from being a real framework. Publishing now would be embarrassing.

**SPECULATIVE BUT LEGITIMATE** — The core insight has value. The formalization is incomplete but the direction is defensible. Could be published as a "perspectives" or "vision" paper with heavy caveats.

**SURPRISINGLY SUBSTANTIVE** — Against expectations, there is real mathematical content here that survives scrutiny. Specific parts deserve further development.
