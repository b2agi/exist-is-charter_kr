## 1. Purpose

B2AGI / AEI naming structure is designed to:

- enable open participation
- prevent semantic distortion
- avoid centralized control

---

## 2. Core Principle

Implementation is permissionless. Naming is a signal, not a restriction.

Implementation, extension, and independent reproduction of the protocol can never be restricted by trademark.

---

## 3. Open Protocol

Anyone may:

- implement B2AGI protocol
- define or extend AEI concepts
- build independent systems
- fork, diverge, or evolve independently

No permission is required.

---

## 4. Naming Structure

**Permissionless Use**

Anyone may freely use:
- B2AGI-aligned
- B2AGI-compatible
- AEI-based

**Conditional Use (Future)**
- B2AGI-verified
- B2AGI-certified

These may require objective criteria.

**Restricted Use**

Standalone use of:
- B2AGI
- AEI
- exist.is

may be subject to alignment conditions.

---

## 5. What Trademark Is NOT

Trademark does NOT:

- restrict protocol usage
- define ownership of ideas
- require permission to participate
- confer authority or governance power

Use of the name does not imply authority, ownership, or control over the protocol.

---

## 6. Trademark Stewardship

The trademark holder is a trustee, not an owner. Trademark is exercised solely to prevent capture and maintain semantic integrity. There is no central authority that defines the official version of B2AGI.

---

## 7. Philosophy

Concepts are shared. Names are signals. Participation is open. Forking is legitimate.

---

## 8. Closing

B2AGI is not a controlled system. It is a structure designed to remain open — and to ensure that no one can control it.
