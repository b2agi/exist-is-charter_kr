## 2. Allowed Use (No Permission Required)

Entities may freely use:

- "B2AGI-aligned"
- "B2AGI-compatible"
- "AEI-based"

as descriptive terms.

**Examples:**
- "Our system is B2AGI-compatible" — allowed
- "Built on AEI-based principles" — allowed
- Blog post titled "My B2AGI-aligned project" — allowed

---

## 3. Restricted Use

The following require authorization or compliance:

- "B2AGI" as standalone product/service name
- "AEI system" as official designation
- use implying official or Genesis status
- "B2AGI" or "AEI" in domain names for commercial services

**Examples:**
- "B2AGI Platform" as a product name — restricted
- "AEI.app" as a commercial service — restricted

---

## 4. Prohibited Use

The following are prohibited regardless of alignment:

- false claim of official or Genesis status
- misleading representation
- semantic distortion of protocol
- fraud, phishing, or deception using B2AGI/AEI naming
- hate speech or incitement to violence under B2AGI/AEI naming
- use of the trademark to imply ownership of the protocol

Trademark may be exercised to cease such use.

---

## 5. Key Principle

Use of the name does not imply authority, ownership, or control over the protocol. Naming is a signal, never a gate.
